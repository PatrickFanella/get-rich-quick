# Cited Papers & References

## Asset Pricing & Factor Models

- **Black, F., Jensen, M.C. & Scholes, M. (1972)**. "The Capital Asset Pricing Model: Some Empirical Tests." _Studies in the Theory of Capital Markets_. — First documented the flat empirical Security Market Line, challenging CAPM. Cited in [[low-volatility-anomaly]].

- **Fama, E.F. & French, K.R. (1992)**. "The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns." _Journal of Finance_, 47(2), 427-465. — Showed book-to-market captures return variation better than beta. Cited in [[value-investing]], [[factor-investing]].

- **Fama, E.F. & French, K.R. (1993)**. "Common Risk Factors in the Returns on Stocks and Bonds." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 33(1), 3-56. — Introduced the three-factor model (MKT, SMB, HML). Cited in [[factor-investing]].

- **Carhart, M.M. (1997)**. "On Persistence in Mutual Fund Performance." _Journal of Finance_. — Added momentum as fourth factor. Cited in [[factor-investing]], [[momentum]].

- **Fama, E.F. & French, K.R. (2015)**. "A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 116(1), 1-22. — Added profitability (RMW) and investment (CMA); explains 71-94% of cross-sectional variance. Cited in [[factor-investing]].

- **Fama, E.F. & French, K.R. (2021)**. "The Value Premium." _Review of Asset Pricing Studies_. — Acknowledged declining value premium in recent decades. Cited in [[value-investing]].

## Momentum

- **Jegadeesh, N. & Titman, S. (1993)**. "Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency." _Journal of Finance_, 48(1), 65-91. — Foundational momentum paper; ~1-1.5% per month excess returns. Cited in [[momentum]].

- **Jegadeesh, N. & Titman, S. (2001)**. "Profitability of Momentum Strategies: An Evaluation of Alternative Explanations." _Journal of Finance_. — Out-of-sample confirmation of 1993 findings. Cited in [[momentum]].

- **Rouwenhorst, K.G. (1998)**. "International Momentum Strategies." _Journal of Finance_. — Confirmed momentum across 12 European markets. Cited in [[momentum]].

- **Asness, C.S., Moskowitz, T.J. & Pedersen, L.H. (2013)**. "Value and Momentum Everywhere." _Journal of Finance_, 68(3), 929-985. — Momentum and value premia across 8 diverse markets; -0.41 correlation between them. Cited in [[momentum]], [[value-investing]], [[strategy-diversification]].

- **Daniel, K. & Moskowitz, T.J. (2016)**. "Momentum Crashes." _Journal of Financial Economics_. — Documented momentum crashes during bear market rebounds. Cited in [[momentum]].

- **Barroso, P. & Santa-Clara, P. (2015)**. "Momentum Has Its Moments." _Journal of Financial Economics_. — Risk-managed momentum doubles Sharpe from 0.53 to 0.97. Cited in [[momentum]].

## Behavioral Finance & Overreaction

- **Hong, H. & Stein, J.C. (1999)**. "A Unified Theory of Underreaction, Momentum Trading, and Overreaction in Asset Markets." _Journal of Finance_. — Modeled gradual information diffusion causing underreaction. Cited in [[momentum]], [[behavioral-biases-in-markets]].

- **Barberis, N., Shleifer, A. & Vishny, R.W. (1998)**. "A Model of Investor Sentiment." _Journal of Financial Economics_. — Conservatism bias causes investors to underweight new evidence. Cited in [[momentum]], [[behavioral-biases-in-markets]].

- **De Bondt, W.F.M. & Thaler, R.H. (1985)**. "Does the Stock Market Overreact?" _Journal of Finance_, 40(3), 793-805. — Prior 3-5 year losers outperform winners; investor overreaction. Cited in [[mean-reversion]], [[behavioral-biases-in-markets]].

- **Lakonishok, J., Shleifer, A. & Vishny, R.W. (1994)**. "Contrarian Investment, Extrapolation, and Risk." _Journal of Finance_, 49(5), 1541-1578. — Growth rate extrapolation causes mispricing of value and glamour stocks. Cited in [[value-investing]], [[behavioral-biases-in-markets]].

## Value & Quality

- **Basu, S. (1977)**. "Investment Performance of Common Stocks in Relation to Their Price-Earnings Ratios." _Journal of Finance_. — Early evidence that low P/E stocks outperform. Cited in [[value-investing]].

- **Graham, B. & Dodd, D. (1934)**. _Security Analysis_. — Codified value investing and "margin of safety." Cited in [[value-investing]].

- **Goncalves, A.S. & Stulz, R.M. (2023)**. "Fundamental-to-Market Ratio and the Value Premium Decline." _Journal of Financial Economics_. — Explains apparent value decline through intangible asset growth. Cited in [[value-investing]].

- **Novy-Marx, R. (2013)**. "The Other Side of Value: The Gross Profitability Premium." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 108(1), 1-28. — Gross profitability predicts returns as strongly as book-to-market. Cited in [[quality-investing]], [[factor-investing]].

- **Asness, C.S., Frazzini, A. & Pedersen, L.H. (2019)**. "Quality Minus Junk." _Review of Accounting Studies_, 24(1), 34-112. — QMJ factor earns risk-adjusted returns in 23 of 24 countries; "puzzlingly modest" price of quality. Cited in [[quality-investing]].

## Trend Following & Managed Futures

- **Moskowitz, T.J., Ooi, Y.H. & Pedersen, L.H. (2012)**. "Time Series Momentum." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 104(2), 228-250. — Significant time-series momentum across all 58 liquid futures studied. Cited in [[trend-following]].

- **Hurst, B., Ooi, Y.H. & Pedersen, L.H. (2017)**. "A Century of Evidence on Trend-Following Investing." _Journal of Portfolio Management_, 44(1), 15-29. — Positive returns in every decade since 1880; crisis alpha in 8 of 10 worst drawdowns. Cited in [[trend-following]].

- **Hurst, B., Ooi, Y.H. & Pedersen, L.H. (2013)**. "Demystifying Managed Futures." _Journal of Investment Management_. — CTA returns explained by TSMOM; gross Sharpe ~1.8. Cited in [[trend-following]].

## Mean Reversion & Statistical Arbitrage

- **Jegadeesh, N. (1990)**. "Evidence of Predictable Behavior of Security Returns." _Journal of Finance_, 45(3), 881-898. — Short-term negative serial correlation; contrarian profits ~2% per month. Cited in [[mean-reversion]].

- **Fama, E.F. & French, K.R. (1988)**. "Permanent and Temporary Components of Stock Prices." _Journal of Political Economy_. — Mean-reverting component accounts for 25-40% of 3-5 year return variance. Cited in [[mean-reversion]].

- **Poterba, J.M. & Summers, L.H. (1988)**. "Mean Reversion in Stock Prices." _Journal of Financial Economics_. — Variance ratio tests confirming mean reversion across US and 17 countries. Cited in [[mean-reversion]].

- **Lo, A.W. & MacKinlay, A.C. (1990)**. "When Are Contrarian Profits Due to Stock Market Overreaction?" _Review of Financial Studies_. — Contrarian profits partly from lead-lag effects, not purely overreaction. Cited in [[mean-reversion]].

- **Engle, R.F. & Granger, C.W.J. (1987)**. "Co-Integration and Error Correction." _Econometrica_. — Nobel Prize-winning cointegration framework; foundation for pairs trading. Cited in [[statistical-arbitrage]].

- **Gatev, E., Goetzmann, W.N. & Rouwenhorst, K.G. (2006)**. "Pairs Trading: Performance of a Relative-Value Arbitrage Rule." _Review of Financial Studies_, 19(3), 797-827. — Top pairs generated ~11% annualized excess returns (1962-2002). Cited in [[statistical-arbitrage]].

- **Avellaneda, M. & Lee, J.-H. (2010)**. "Statistical Arbitrage in the US Equities Market." _Quantitative Finance_. — PCA-based stat arb achieving Sharpe of 1.44. Cited in [[statistical-arbitrage]].

- **Do, B. & Faff, R. (2010)**. "Does Simple Pairs Trading Still Work?" _Financial Analysts Journal_. — Documented decline in pairs trading profitability. Cited in [[statistical-arbitrage]].

## Carry Trade

- **Koijen, R.S.J., Moskowitz, T.J., Pedersen, L.H. & Vrugt, E.B. (2018)**. "Carry." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 127(2), 197-225. — Carry predicts returns across equities, bonds, commodities, credit, options; global Sharpe ~0.9. Cited in [[carry-trade]].

- **Lustig, H. & Verdelhan, A. (2007)**. "The Cross Section of Foreign Currency Risk Premia and Consumption Growth Risk." _American Economic Review_. — Carry returns compensate for consumption growth risk. Cited in [[carry-trade]].

- **Brunnermeier, M.K., Nagel, S. & Pedersen, L.H. (2009)**. "Carry Trades and Currency Crashes." _NBER Macroeconomics Annual_. — Negative skewness from sudden unwinds; VIX predicts carry losses. Cited in [[carry-trade]].

## Volatility & Options

- **Coval, J.D. & Shumway, T. (2001)**. "Expected Option Returns." _Journal of Finance_, 56(3), 983-1009. — Zero-beta ATM straddles produce ~3% weekly losses for option buyers. Cited in [[volatility-selling]].

- **Bakshi, G. & Kapadia, N. (2003)**. "Delta-Hedged Gains and the Negative Market Volatility Risk Premium." _Review of Financial Studies_. — Delta-hedged portfolios systematically underperform zero; VRP ~16% of call prices. Cited in [[volatility-selling]].

- **Ilmanen, A. (2012)**. "Do Financial Markets Reward Buying or Selling Insurance and Lottery Tickets?" _Financial Analysts Journal_. — Selling insurance systematically rewarded; buying lottery tickets systematically costly. Cited in [[volatility-selling]], [[strategy-diversification]].

## Low-Volatility Anomaly

- **Ang, A., Hodrick, R.J., Xing, Y. & Zhang, X. (2006)**. "The Cross-Section of Volatility and Expected Returns." _Journal of Finance_, 61(1), 259-299. — High idiosyncratic volatility stocks earn "abysmally low" returns. Cited in [[low-volatility-anomaly]].

- **Frazzini, A. & Pedersen, L.H. (2014)**. "Betting Against Beta." _Journal of Financial Economics_, 111(1), 1-25. — Leverage constraints explain low-vol anomaly; BAB factor significant across 20+ markets. Cited in [[low-volatility-anomaly]], [[behavioral-biases-in-markets]].

- **Baker, M., Bradley, B. & Wurgler, J. (2011)**. "Benchmarks as Limits to Arbitrage: Understanding the Low-Volatility Anomaly." _Financial Analysts Journal_. — Called it "the greatest anomaly in finance"; benchmark constraints prevent arbitrage. Cited in [[low-volatility-anomaly]].

## Market Making

- **Avellaneda, M. & Stoikov, S. (2008)**. "High-Frequency Trading in a Limit Order Book." _Quantitative Finance_. — Optimal market-making model based on inventory and market activity. Cited in [[market-making]].

## Risk Parity

- **Filho, A.C. & Gaspar, R.M. (2024)**. "Risk Parity Portfolio Performance." _Journal of Portfolio Management_. — Risk parity vs mean-variance (1990-2019); works well short-term, less clear over 20 years. Cited in [[risk-parity]].

## LLM Trading & AI Finance

- **TradingAgents (2024)**. UCLA/MIT. arXiv:2412.20138. — Multi-agent LLM framework; 26.6% returns on Apple in 3-month backtest. Cited in [[multi-agent-trading-systems]], [[llm-bot-architecture]].

- **FinMem (2024)**. Accepted at ICLR Workshop. — Layered short/medium/long-term memory aligned with human trader cognition. Cited in [[llm-bot-architecture]].

- **CryptoTrade (2024)**. EMNLP 2024. — Reflective agent analyzing prior trade outcomes for crypto. Cited in [[llm-bot-architecture]].

- **FINSABER (2025)**. — Most comprehensive LLM trading benchmark; 20+ years, 100+ symbols; previously reported advantages deteriorate significantly. Cited in [[llm-strategy-limitations]].

- **StockBench (2025)**. — Confirmed most LLM agents struggle to beat buy-and-hold. Cited in [[llm-strategy-limitations]].

- **FS-ReasoningAgent**. — Factual + subjective LLM parallel analysis for crypto; 7-10% higher profits. Cited in [[multi-agent-trading-systems]].

- **MarketSenseAI 2.0 (2024)**. — 77-78% win rate, alpha 8.0-18.9% on S&P 500. Cited in [[sentiment-analysis-trading]].

- **Lopez-Lira, A. & Tang, Y. (2023)**. "Can ChatGPT Forecast Stock Price Movements?" University of Florida. — ChatGPT sentiment scores significantly predict daily returns; 400%+ cumulative return. Cited in [[sentiment-analysis-trading]].

- **Kim, S. et al. (2024)**. Financial news sentiment study (965,375 articles). — OPT model 74.4% accuracy, Sharpe 3.05. Cited in [[sentiment-analysis-trading]].
