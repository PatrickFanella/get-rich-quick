# Quality Investing

Targets firms with high profitability, stable earnings, low leverage, and strong fundamentals.

## Key Discoveries

### Gross Profitability (Novy-Marx, 2013)

**Novy-Marx (2013, JFE 108(1), 1-28)**: Gross profitability (revenue minus COGS, scaled by assets) predicts returns with roughly the same power as book-to-market. Because profitability and [[value-investing|value]] are negatively correlated, combining both produces more consistent returns. This directly motivated the RMW factor in the [[factor-investing|Fama-French five-factor model]].

### Quality Minus Junk (Asness, Frazzini & Pedersen, 2019)

**Asness, Frazzini & Pedersen (2019, RAS 24(1), 34-112)**: Formalized QMJ as a composite of profitability, growth, safety, and payout.

- Significant risk-adjusted returns in **23 of 24 countries**
- "Puzzlingly modest" price of quality -- high-quality stocks only modestly more expensive despite substantially superior fundamentals
- Performs well during market downturns
- Negative exposure to market, value, and size factors
- Extremely difficult to explain through standard risk models
- Authors conclude: "We cannot tie the returns of quality to risk"

## Why the Anomaly Persists

The quality premium is particularly puzzling because:

1. Quality stocks are transparently better businesses
2. They don't carry obvious risk exposure
3. The premium exists across nearly all countries studied
4. It may relate to [[behavioral-biases-in-markets|investor inattention to fundamentals]] vs. price signals
