# Risk vs Mispricing Debate

The behavioral-versus-risk debate runs through every systematic strategy. The truth likely involves elements of both.

## The Two Sides

### Risk Compensation (Rational)

Factor premia exist because they compensate investors for bearing genuine economic risks:

- **[[value-investing|Value]]**: Value stocks are distressed, riskier firms (Fama & French, 1992)
- **[[carry-trade|Carry]]**: Compensates for consumption growth risk during recessions (Lustig & Verdelhan, 2007)
- **[[volatility-selling|Volatility selling]]**: Premium for bearing crash/tail risk
- **[[low-volatility-anomaly|Low-vol]]**: Leverage constraints create structural mispricing (Frazzini & Pedersen, 2014)

### Behavioral Mispricing (Irrational)

Premia arise from systematic cognitive errors that create persistent mispricings:

- **[[value-investing|Value]]**: Investors overextrapolate past growth rates (Lakonishok et al., 1994); value stocks don't underperform in downturns
- **[[momentum]]**: Underreaction to information, disposition effect
- **[[quality-investing|Quality]]**: "Puzzlingly modest" price of quality; authors conclude "we cannot tie the returns of quality to risk"
- **[[low-volatility-anomaly|Low-vol]]**: Lottery preference, benchmark constraints

## Strategy-by-Strategy Assessment

| Strategy                   | Risk explanation            | Behavioral explanation                    | Verdict                  |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| [[momentum]]               | Compensation for crash risk | Underreaction, disposition effect         | Primarily behavioral     |
| [[value-investing]]        | Distress risk               | Extrapolation bias                        | Mixed; weakened recently |
| [[carry-trade]]            | Consumption risk            | Crowding, herding                         | Both compelling          |
| [[volatility-selling]]     | Tail risk compensation      | Insurance demand, fear premium            | Both                     |
| [[low-volatility-anomaly]] | N/A (contradicts CAPM)      | Lottery preference + leverage constraints | Structural + behavioral  |
| [[quality-investing]]      | Hard to identify            | Inattention to fundamentals               | Primarily behavioral     |
| [[trend-following]]        | Risk transfer from hedgers  | Anchoring, herding                        | Structural + behavioral  |

## Practical Implication

The debate doesn't need to be resolved for practitioners. Both explanations suggest the premia are durable: risk premia persist by definition (investors need compensation), and behavioral biases persist because they're rooted in human psychology.
