# Strategy Diversification

The most actionable insight from decades of research: **diversifying across strategy styles -- not just asset classes -- is the most reliable path to improved portfolio efficiency.**

## Key Negative Correlations

| Strategy Pair                                                              | Correlation | Implication                                                         |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[value-investing\|Value]] & [[momentum\|Momentum]]                        | **-0.41**   | Combining both dramatically improves risk-adjusted returns          |
| [[trend-following\|Trend following]] & [[carry-trade\|Carry]]              | Negative    | Opposite crisis profiles: trend gains during crashes, carry suffers |
| [[trend-following\|Trend following]] & [[volatility-selling\|Vol selling]] | Negative    | Same crisis dynamic                                                 |
| [[quality-investing\|Quality]] & Market/Value/Size                         | Negative    | QMJ provides independent return stream                              |

## Insurance Profile Spectrum

Strategies fall on a spectrum from "insurance seller" to "insurance buyer":

### Insurance Sellers (Steady gains, infrequent large losses)

- [[carry-trade]]
- [[volatility-selling]]
- [[low-volatility-anomaly]] (partially)

### Insurance Buyers (Costly in calm markets, profitable in crises)

- [[trend-following]] -- "crisis alpha"

Combining both profiles produces a smoother return stream.

## Ilmanen's Framework

Harvest multiple independent risk premia:

1. Value
2. Momentum
3. Carry
4. Volatility
5. Defensive/Quality

Each captures a different source of returns, and their low correlations mean the portfolio Sharpe ratio exceeds any individual strategy's Sharpe.
